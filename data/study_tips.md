# Exam Format & Logistics

The **SHV/FSVL Theorieprüfung Gleitschirm** is the Swiss paragliding theory exam administered by the Schweizerischer Hängegleiter Verband (Swiss Hang Gliding and Paragliding Federation).

- **Total questions: 100** — 20 questions from each of 5 subject areas.
- **Five sections:**
  1. **Flugpraxis** (Flight Practice)
  2. **Meteorologie** (Meteorology)
  3. **Aerodynamik / Flugtheorie** (Aerodynamics / Flight Theory)
  4. **Materialkunde** (Equipment Knowledge)
  5. **Luftrecht** (Legislation / Air Law)
- **Time limit:** 90 minutes maximum.
- **Format:** Multiple choice, 4 answers per question.
- **Passing score: 80% per section** — you may have a **maximum of 4 errors per subject area**. Failing even one section means failing the exam, so balanced studying matters.
- **Languages:** German, French, Italian, English (same as the E-Learning).
- **Where:** In-person, registered through the SHV online portal (login required, must be SHV member or registered).
- **No calculator allowed.**
- **Cost:** See the "Gebührenreglement SHV ab 2026" PDF on the SHV site. From Jan 1, 2026 onwards, license extensions/reissues are bundled into the exam fee. Older references quote around CHF 125 per attempt; confirm with the current fee schedule.
- **Prerequisite to practical exam:** Pass theory + 50 altitude flights at 5+ different sites + flight instructor sign-off.
- **Bring:** Completed Kontrollblatt, ID, your (and any borrowed equipment's) insurance certificate.

# Common Pitfalls

- **Meteorology questions are NOT word-for-word from the E-Learning.** The SHV reformulates them so you cannot just memorize — you need to actually understand convection, stability, Föhn detection, and cloud development.
- **Failing one section = failing the whole exam.** Many candidates over-study their favorite topic (often aerodynamics) and underprepare for Luftrecht or Materialkunde.
- **Föhn detection** trips people up: signs are deep blue sky, abnormally warm temperatures, strong winds aloft while ground is calm, lenticular clouds, sharp "Föhnmauer" cloud wall on the windward side.
- **Polar curve interpretation:** know the difference between Minimum Sink (slowest descent rate, lightly braked) and Best Glide (best L/D, trim speed) — many confuse them. Stall Point is at maximum brake.
- **Airspace** is the highest-stakes topic: you must know which classes paragliders may enter without clearance (G and E in Switzerland) vs. those requiring clearance (C, D, CTR, TMA).
- **Drag vs. speed is non-linear** — drag grows with the square of speed. A common trick question.
- **Cumulonimbus** updrafts/downdrafts/gust fronts vastly exceed paraglider speed — never fly near them.
- **Variable oral questions on practical exam (post-Apr 2025):** examiners may now ask about meteorology or flight practice as well as airspace. Theory study compounds into practical prep.

# Study Strategy

- **Typical prep time:** ~2 weeks at ~1 hour/day if your school's theory course is fresh. If you're rusty or starting cold, plan 4–6 weeks.
- **Primary resource: SHV E-Learning (elearning.shv-fsvl.ch).** Free for members. Two modes — Practice (filter by unanswered, by topic) and Exam (full simulation). Do at least 2 full passes; meteorology questions may differ on exam day, but everything else is drawn from the same pool.
- **Run the question catalog top-to-bottom twice.** Forum consensus is that this alone is usually enough to pass.
- **Use Anki/Quizlet for hard facts** (airspace minimums, frequencies, insurance amounts). Spaced repetition crushes the rote-memory questions.
- **Textbook backup:** "Gleitschirmfliegen" — the standard Swiss reference. Flugschule Freewings provides free video lectures (5 modules × 2 parts) at freewings.ch/shv-theoriekurs.
- **For tricky topics:** read soaringmeteo.org's free SHV exam commentary PDFs (parts 1–3) — they explain *why* answers are correct, which the E-Learning does not.
- **Memorize vs. understand:**
  - **Memorize cold:** airspace tables, cloud distances, FL/feet conversions, MIL-ON/OFF times, emergency frequency, insurance minimums, standard pressure.
  - **Understand:** polar curve behavior, Föhn formation, thermal development, lift/drag relationships, weight-shift mechanics, certification classes (EN A/B/C/D).
- **Airspace drill:** open the SHV airspace map (airspace.shv-fsvl.ch) and pick random points — name the classes from ground up. This is the same test the practical examiner gives.

# Must-Memorize Cheat Sheet

**Standard atmosphere (ISA):**
- 1013.25 hPa at MSL, 15 °C, lapse rate 6.5 °C / 1000 m (≈2 °C / 1000 ft).
- 1 hPa ≈ 8.3 m ≈ 27 ft near sea level.

**Conversions:**
- **FL × 30 ≈ meters** (e.g. FL100 ≈ 3050 m, FL130 ≈ 3950 m, FL150 ≈ 4550 m).
- **FL × 100 = feet** (FL100 = 10,000 ft).
- 1 m ≈ 3.28 ft. 1 km/h ≈ 0.54 kt.

**Swiss airspace (paraglider-relevant):**
- **Class G (uncontrolled):** ground to 600 m AGL (outside CTRs). Need: clear of clouds + ground in sight; 1.5 km visibility.
- **Class E Mittelland/Jura:** 600 m AGL → FL100. VFR: 5 km vis, 1500 m horizontal / 300 m vertical from clouds.
- **Class E Alps:** 600 m AGL → FL130 (MIL-ON) or FL150 (MIL-OFF). Vis: 5 km below FL100, 8 km above.
- **Class C, D, CTR, TMA:** clearance required (effectively no-go for paragliders without ATC contact).
- **LS-R (Restricted):** entry forbidden when active. **LS-D (Danger):** legally allowed but not recommended.
- **RMZ:** radio contact required. **TMZ:** transponder required.

**MIL-ON / MIL-OFF (military flight times in Class E Alps):**
- **MIL-ON: Mon–Fri 07:30–12:05 LT and 13:15–17:05 LT** → ceiling FL130.
- **MIL-OFF:** all other times incl. weekends + listed Swiss public holidays (New Year, Berchtold, Good Friday, Easter Monday, Ascension, Whit Monday, 1 Aug, 24/25/26 Dec, 31 Dec) → ceiling FL150.

**Radio frequencies:**
- **Emergency / Rega: 161.300 MHz** (Switzerland-wide, monitored by Rega). License-free, max 5 W.
- **Air-band (aviation VHF):** 118.000–136.975 MHz — for ATC contact.

**Insurance minimums (Switzerland):**
- **Solo pilot liability: CHF 1 million minimum** (mandatory). SHV standard policy covers CHF 10 million for CHF 82.50/yr.
- **Tandem passenger liability (mandatory since 2020): CHF 5 million.** Non-commercial CHF 257/yr; commercial CHF 967/yr.

**Cloud clearance / VFR minimums in controlled airspace (C/D/E):**
- **1500 m horizontal, 300 m vertical** from clouds.
- 5 km visibility (8 km in E above FL100).

**Polar curve key points (in order of brake input from 0 to full):**
1. **Trim / Best Glide** — best L/D ratio, lowest brake.
2. **Minimum Sink** — slowest descent, more brake.
3. **Stall Point** — full brake, wing stops flying.

# Test-Day Tips

- **Time budget:** 90 min / 100 questions = ~54 sec each. Plenty of time — don't rush, but don't sit on one question.
- **First pass: answer everything you know cold.** Mark uncertain ones and return after.
- **Per-section pacing matters:** since you can only afford 4 errors *per section*, don't burn all uncertainty into one topic.
- **Read the German/French/Italian carefully** if not your first language — SHV is fond of double-negatives and "welche Aussage ist FALSCH?" (which statement is FALSE?) phrasings. Underline the keyword.
- **Eliminate obviously wrong answers first.** With 4 options, removing 2 doubles your odds.
- **For meteorology word-problems**, sketch the situation (terrain, wind, sun) on scratch paper — patterns become obvious.
- **No calculator.** Practice FL→m and hPa→m conversions in your head beforehand.
- **Trust the E-Learning.** If a question feels weird, the "textbook" Swiss answer is usually the safest — don't overthink with real-world pilot lore.
- **Arrive early**, bring ID + Kontrollblatt + insurance certificate. Eat first; 90 min concentration on an empty stomach is brutal.
- **If you fail a section, you typically only re-sit that section** — confirm with your flight school, but this means a bad day isn't a total restart.
